Quote

Biography

Renowned Indian scientist and former President, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in the coastal town of Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Kalam's early fascination with flight and science can be traced back to his childhood, where he grew up listening to stories about World War II and the simplicity of his parents' lives. His father, Jainulabdeen, was an imam and a boat owner, while his mother, Ashiamma, was a dedicated housewife. After completing his schooling at Schwartz Higher Secondary School, Ramanathapuram, Kalam went on to study physics at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. Later, he shifted his focus to aerospace engineering, attending the Madras Institute of Technology. This foundation set the stage for his future contributions to India's space and defense sectors.
